Consent

Soulify requires your permission to enable user scopes (such as 'playlist-modify-public,' which enables the creation of playlists and add music on your behalf) in order to utilize  functionalities within the Spotify API. We do not keep or distribute any of your personal data. Permission can be rescinded with the following link.

Top Tracks

There is no way to see your most listened to tracks without using Spotify Wrapped, which can only be seen at end of the year. This feature is provided by TopTracks, which allows you to generate a playlist of your favorite music from the previous month, six months, or all time.

Create

Soulify offers a unique playlist creation tool that mixes music based on tunable attributes. To begin, select up to 5 song titles or artists that you wish to be featured in the playlist. Name your playlist and then set the tunable attributes that you wish to emphasize or eliminate from your playlist.  The resulting playlist will be unaffected by non-selected qualities.

FAQ

Is it secure?

Soulify adheres to all modern standards in web security. And as logging in to Soulify is handled by Spotify, it's as secure as logging in to Spotify itself.

How is my data used?

To use Soulify, you'll need to allow the app to have access to some of your account information - that being your Spotify user ID, the country your account is registered to, and a list of your top artists & tracks. Your data is only used to calculate an average with which users can compare their own scores.

Where does the artist and track ranking come from?

The content and order of your lists come directly from Spotify's API. Popularity, genres, and musical traits are all determined by Spotify.

Why are there no (or few) results showing up in my lists?

If you listen in Private Mode, those songs won't show up in either list. If your account is new, it's normal for your All-Time list to be short, as it takes some time (one to two weeks) for Spotify to update their information.
